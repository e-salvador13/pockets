## What YNAB Does Well
- "Cost to Be Me" framing is genius — not "budget" but identity
- Progress bars with deadlines create urgency without anxiety
- "No shame, no guilt" tone on wants
- Interactive demo during onboarding = aha moment before commitment
- 5-step post-onboarding checklist = clear activation path
- Net Worth tracking built in
- Bank sync via MX is smooth
- Easy to add custom categories (Eduardo added Hair Cut, Coffee, Shopping)

## What's Weak (CashPilot Opportunities)
- **It's a quiz, not a conversation.** 20 screens of checkboxes, no emotional depth
- **Motivation question is wasted.** Same flow whether "get out of debt" or "make the most of money"
- **No archetype identification.** Everyone gets the same experience
- **No AI anywhere.** Manual transaction categorization, no insights surfaced automatically
- **First day is underwhelming.** Reflect tab is empty, Age of Money needs 10 transactions
- **"Stuff I forgot to plan for" = instant failure.** Red alerts on day 1 feel bad
- **Heavy manual setup.** Need to add targets to every category individually
- **No "holy shit" data moment.** Your own data is just a transaction list, no surprising comparisons
- **The 3-month emotional shift is too slow.** Your coworker said it took 3 months to "feel differently"
- **No proactive guidance.** App is passive — doesn't reach out, doesn't suggest, doesn't coach

## CashPilot Differentiators to Build
1. **Conversational onboarding** — 5 AI turns instead of 20 checkbox screens
2. **Money archetype detection** — Ramit Sethi style (warrior/optimizer/avoider)
3. **Instant data insight** — connect bank → "you spent more at X than Y" within first session
4. **AI categorization** — auto-tag transactions, learn your patterns
5. **Proactive coaching** — reach out when you overspend, approaching targets
6. **"Roll with the punches" automation** — when surprise expense hits, AI rebalances your plan
7. **Compress the 3-month shift** — emotional connection in days, not months
